The Great Pyramid of Giza

The Largest Egyptian Pyramid

The Great Pyramids of Giza is the largest Egyptian pyramid and the tomb of the Fourth Dynasty pharaoh Khufu. Built in the 26th century BC during a period of around 27 years, it is the oldest of the Seven Wonders of the Ancient World, and the only on to remain largely intact. As part of the Giza Pyramid complex, it borders present-day Giza in Greater Cairo, Egypt.

The Great Sphinx of Giza

The Limestone Statue of Legend

The Great Sphinx of Giza is a limestone statue of a reclinging sphinx, a mythical creature with the head of a human and the body a lion. Facing directly from the west to the east, it stands on the Giza Plateau on the west bank of the Nile in Giza, Egypt. The face of the Sphinx appears to represent the pharoah Khafre.
